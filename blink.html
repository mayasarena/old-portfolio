Overview

During my final year at Western University, we were tasked with creating a large-scale video game from scratch. Our team designed and developed Blink, a first-person shooter that allows the player to freeze time during gameplay. In addition to this unique feature, Blink includes various other mechanics such as running, sliding, jumping, picking up items and weapons, and reloading weapons.

My Role

As the artistic lead of the team, one of my responsibilities was to develop the game's overall visual design. We decided on a glow-in-the-dark theme, and I created simple, glowing game assets that my team members could use within their levels to keep the visuals consistent. In addition to this, I was in charge of implementing and finding game assets for the game's user interface, which included elements such as minimaps, a health bar, the Blink mechanic bar, and all menus.

Each team member was responsible for designing and implementing a specific level, and I was tasked with creating the tutorial level. Throughout the year, I was actively involved in all meetings and milestone presentations, where I provided updates on the progress of the game's design elements and my own implementations.
