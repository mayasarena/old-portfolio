Overview

Background Info

This personal project emerged from the shared struggles of friends and personal frustrations during the job search after graduation. The challenges included difficulties in finding relevant job postings and feeling unprepared for the transition from academia to the professional world. Recognizing the inadequacies of current job search platforms and the gap in educational preparation, the aim was to create a comprehensive solution to help students and new graduates.

My Role

Prototyper, Researcher, UX Designer - Independent personal project

Design Process

Defining the problem

Market Research

I conducted a survey involving five participants to identify pain points in the job search process. Responses were analyzed to gain insights into the issues users face and their suggested solutions. The focus was on understanding the shortcomings of existing job posting platforms and the overwhelming feelings associated with the initial job search.

The Problem

Current job posting websites fall short for students and new grads due to inaccuracies and a lack of transparency in job postings. Users often feel overwhelmed, unprepared, and underqualified during their job search. The demand for more reliable job postings, enhanced filtering options, and tools to better prepare for job searches became evident through user feedback.

Understanding the users

User Persona

A user persona, named Daniel, was crafted to represent the typical new grad experiencing anxiety and a lack of experience directly after completing their degree. Recognizing the real-world challenges faced by graduates, such as having no internship or co-op experience due to financial constraints and mental health issues, was crucial in shaping Daniel's persona.

User Journey Map

A user journey map was developed to highlight Daniel's pain points during the job search. This map served as a visual guide to identify areas of improvement and additional features that could enhance the application's effectiveness.

Conclusions

Key Learnings

01
User Personas and User Journey Maps are key! User personas and journey maps proved instrumental in empathizing with the user's perspective and identifying areas for improvement. Despite a limited participant pool, these tools provided valuable insights.
02
Sometimes you can't solve the users problem - and that's okay! Acknowledging that certain issues, such as the availability of job postings, may be beyond the app's control was an essential realization. Designers can focus on facilitating existing processes, making it easier for users to navigate available opportunities.
03
Survey questions need to be clear. Clear survey questions are vital for accurate participant responses. A participant's misunderstanding outlined the importance of framing questions with precision, especially in a survey format where clarification isn't immediately available.

Conclusion

Motivated by personal frustrations and the shared experiences of peers, this project aimed to address the challenges encountered by students and recent graduates in their job search. By adopting a user-centric approach, incorporating valuable feedback, and recognizing the project's limitations, the path toward creating a comprehensive job search solution was insightful and purposeful. Moving forward, the next phase of this project would include conducting usability studies and refining the initial application design through iterative processes.
